Rack Cable Managments

Rack cable management (RCM) is one of the most pressing challenges encountered by most network installers and IT technicians in server rooms and data centers. It contains six different parts: horizontal cable management, vertical cable management, overhead cable tray systems, copper or fiber patch panel, labeling and cable ties. These parts are combined together to create an innovative and complete system for patch cable management. Today’s topic will focus on vertical rack cable management.

VERTICAL CABLE MANAGEMENT & ACCESSORIES

The Vertical Cable Management mounts between standard 7' equipment racks to provide neat and orderly routing of cables and jumpers. Each Vertical Cable Management consists of two covers, front and rear. The fingers in the channel are spaced to provide easy entrance and exit of cables while still maintaining control of the twisted pair’s cable bend radii.

HORIZONTAL CABLE MANAGEMENT

The cable management system provides a neat and efficient means for routing and protecting cables on standard EIA 19" racks. The cable management system keeps your cable organized yet accessible so that moves, add and changes are easier than ever before. These components are part of an integrated system of products including patch panel, racks and patch cords, designed to satisfy your current and future telecommunication closet cabling needs.
